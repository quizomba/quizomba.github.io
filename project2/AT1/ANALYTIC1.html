Analytic Trail 1

How does women's right relate to acceptance of homosexuality?

In this project I will investigate how homosexuals acceptance and women’s right interact in a society. In order to do that, I crossed 8 variables avaiable in the World Value Survey database. I cleaned and treated the data, finding quotients, so I had one unique number to compare each country. I also normalized the quotients, so the scale in each different variable is more relatable. Here it's possible to see each variable and what the data is telling.

  • V45 When jobs are scarce, men have a priority.
    Number of people who agree/number of people who disagree. Bigger numbers mean men have a higher priority.

  • v47 There is a problem in women earning more than men
    Number of people who agree/number of people who disagree. Bigger numbers mean women with higher salaries can be a problem.

  • V51 Men make better decision than women politically
    Number of people who agree/number of people who disagree. Bigger numbers mean men make better decisions.

  • V53 Men make better business executive than woman
    Number of people who agree/number of people who disagree. Bigger numbers mean men make better executives.

  • V139 Democracy: women have the same right as men
    % of people who said women participation is crucial for a country democracy.

  • V48 Having a job is the best way for a woman to be independent
    Number of people who agree/number of people who disagree. Bigger numbers mean having job is more relevant for a woman.

  • V40 Would not like to have as neighbors homosexuals
    Number of people who mentioned homosexuals are not good neighbors/number of people who did not mentioned. Bigger numbers mean homosexuality is more accepted.

  • V203 Justifiable: homosexuality
    The mean answer, nn a scale where 0 means homosexuality is never justifiable and 10 means it’s always justifiable.

    • These are the countries in the investigation, and also the time period I am investigating.

  • Wave 1: 1989-1993
  • Wave 2: 1994-1998
  • Wave 3: 1999-2004
  • Wave 4: 2005-2009
  • Wave 5: 2010-2010

    • With this scales, I used Tableau to create parallel coordinates which were used as a tool to find correlations between variables. I created different parallel coordinates for each time period.

    It is important to notice that not every time period has the same data. That is why some of them ends with straight lines, they actually mean lack of data.
    Although the number of countries can make it a little complicated, we can see that there are big variations between the variables. We can find some negative correlations. For example, countries with a higher V40 number (meaning they accept more homosexuals) tend to have a lower v45 number (which means they don't think men have priority in a scarce job market). In other words, we can say that countries tend to be more "progressive" as a hole, that is: if they accept homosexuality, women tend to be more equal as men. It can be that both variables are a measure of "macho" traits in a society, but this needs further investigaion.


    To make it clearer, here we can see paralel coordinates with only 5 countries (Brazil, Iraq, Kuwait, Spain, Sweden).

    With this smaller dataset we can see that there is indeed a correlation. The countries curves are almost inverted, showing that the ones that better accept homosexuality are also the ones where women are more equal to men.
